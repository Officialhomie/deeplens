Zero-knowledge proofs in practice

A zero-knowledge proof lets a prover convince a verifier that a statement is true without revealing why it is true beyond the validity of the claim itself.

Deep mode expands the mental model: commitments, witnesses, and the intuition behind soundness and completeness.

zero-knowledge
📌 ✕

What it is A cryptographic protocol where the verifier learns nothing except that the claim holds.

Mental model Imagine proving you know a password without sending the password—you send only evidence that satisfies the verifier's checks.

Why it matters here The article discusses privacy-preserving verification; ZK is the bridge between "trust the math" and "don't leak the data."
