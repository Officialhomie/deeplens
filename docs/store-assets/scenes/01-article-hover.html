Understanding eigenvalues in linear systems

In many engineering problems, the behavior of a dynamical system is determined by the eigenvalue spectrum of its state matrix. When all eigenvalues lie inside the unit circle, the discrete-time system is stable.

Readers often pause here—the term connects spectral theory, stability proofs, and practical control design in a single concept.

eigenvalue
📌 ✕

What it is A scalar λ such that Av = λv for a square matrix A and nonzero vector v.

In this context Eigenvalues tell you whether small perturbations grow or decay over time—central to stability analysis in the paragraph you're reading.
